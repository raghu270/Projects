Air quality is a critical concern for public health and environmental sustainability. The increasing levels of pollutants in the atmosphere have led to adverse health effects and environmental degradation. Accurate prediction of air quality can play a vital role in mitigating these issues. In this project, we propose a novel approach to predict air quality using machine learning techniques.

The project aims to develop a predictive model that estimates air quality indicators such as particulate matter (PM2.5 and PM10), ozone (O3), nitrogen dioxide (NO2), sulfur dioxide (SO2), and carbon monoxide (CO) levels. To achieve this, historical air quality data from monitoring stations will be collected and preprocessed. Feature engineering techniques will be employed to extract relevant temporal, meteorological, and geographical features that impact air quality.
